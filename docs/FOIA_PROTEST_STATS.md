## (a) Request letter — ready to paste into SecureRelease / FOIA.gov

> **Note:** SecureRelease/FOIA.gov use a web form; paste the body below into the request-description field. Fields in `[BRACKETS]` must be filled in before submission.

```
FREEDOM OF INFORMATION ACT REQUEST

To: FOIA Officer, U.S. Customs and Border Protection

Requester:
[REQUESTER NAME]
[COMPANY: TariffCheck, Inc. — or current legal entity name]
[STREET ADDRESS]
[CITY, STATE ZIP]
[EMAIL]  |  [PHONE]

Date: [DATE]

Re: FOIA Request — Aggregate protest disposition statistics, ACE Protest
Module, FY2022 through FY2026 (to date)

Dear FOIA Officer:

Under the Freedom of Information Act, 5 U.S.C. § 552, I request copies of
existing records — reports, data extracts, dashboards, spreadsheets, or
briefing materials — containing aggregate, anonymized statistics on
administrative protests filed with CBP under 19 U.S.C. § 1514 and
19 C.F.R. Part 174 and processed in the ACE Protest Module (or any
predecessor or successor system of record for protests).

Specifically, for each of fiscal years 2022, 2023, 2024, 2025, and 2026
(year-to-date), I request records showing, to the extent they exist:

  1. The number of protests filed;
  2. The number of protests decided, broken out by disposition:
     granted (approved), denied, granted in part / partially approved,
     rejected as non-protestable, and administratively closed or withdrawn;
  3. Items 1–2 broken out by protest issue category as recorded in the
     ACE Protest Module (e.g., classification, valuation, country of
     origin, exclusion, duty rate/Chapter 99 trade-remedy issues, and any
     other issue codes used by the module);
  4. Items 1–2 broken out by review type: protests subject to an
     Application for Further Review (AFR) under 19 C.F.R. § 174.23 versus
     standard port-level review;
  5. Any tracked processing-time metrics, such as median or average days
     from filing to decision, if such metrics are maintained in reports
     or system extracts.

SCOPE AND FORMAT. To be clear, I am NOT asking CBP to create new records,
conduct new analysis, or compile statistics that do not already exist. I
request only (i) existing reports, extracts, or summaries, and/or (ii) data
that can be produced from the ACE Protest Module or equivalent systems
using existing query and reporting capabilities, which constitutes a
search of an automated system under 5 U.S.C. § 552(a)(3)(C). I request
production in native electronic format (CSV or Excel preferred; PDF
acceptable) under 5 U.S.C. § 552(a)(3)(B).

FALLBACK. If no responsive record matches the exact breakdowns above,
I request the closest existing dataset or report on protest volumes and
dispositions — for example, any recurring Office of Trade or Trade
Transformation Office management report, ACE reports-workspace extract,
performance metric, or congressional/GAO briefing material covering
protest workload and outcomes for FY2022–FY2026. I would welcome a call
to narrow scope if that speeds processing.

EXCLUSIONS (to reduce burden): I do not seek any personally identifiable
information, importer- or filer-identifying information, or the contents
of individual protests. Aggregate counts only.

FEES. This request is made for commercial use, and I accept commercial-
requester fee treatment under 6 C.F.R. § 5.11. I agree to pay applicable
search, review, and duplication fees up to $100.00 without further
authorization. If estimated fees will exceed $100.00, please contact me
with an estimate before incurring costs.

If any portion of this request is denied, please cite the specific FOIA
exemption for each withholding, release all reasonably segregable
non-exempt portions, and advise me of my appeal rights.

Please confirm receipt with a tracking number. I can be reached at
[EMAIL] or [PHONE] with any questions, and I am glad to discuss scope.

Respectfully,

[REQUESTER NAME]
[TITLE], [COMPANY]
```

## (b) Submission checklist

1. **Where to file (pick one):**
   - **CBP SecureRelease portal — preferred:** `https://www.securerelease.us/` → create account → New Request → agency: U.S. Customs and Border Protection → paste letter body. You'll receive an acknowledgment + tracking number and can track status in the portal.
   - **FOIA.gov:** `https://www.foia.gov/` → search "U.S. Customs and Border Protection" → Start request → paste letter body.
   - Do **not** mail/fax/email: CBP stopped accepting hard-copy FOIA requests effective **January 22, 2026**.
2. **Before submitting:** fill all `[BRACKETS]`; use the legal entity name; keep the $100 fee ceiling unless we decide to raise it. No Certification of Identity needed (this is not a first-party records request).
3. **Category selection in the form:** if asked for requester type, select **Commercial**. If asked which CBP office, indicate **Office of Trade / trade records** (protests), not Border Patrol/traveler records.
4. **Expected timeline:** statutory response is **20 business days** (5 U.S.C. §552(a)(6)(A)), extendable 10 more for "unusual circumstances." Realistically, CBP non-first-party commercial requests often take **1–6 months**. Calendar a status check at day 30 via the portal; call (202) 325-0150 if no acknowledgment within a week.
5. **If fees are estimated above $100:** they must contact us first (the letter requires it). Decide then whether to raise the ceiling or narrow to fewer fiscal years.
6. **If "no records" or denial:**
   - First, invoke the fallback paragraph — ask what protest workload reports *do* exist (a phone call with the FOIA analyst is allowed and often productive).
   - **Administrative appeal:** within **90 days** of the final determination, via SecureRelease, or by mail to: FOIA Appeals, Policy and Litigation Branch, U.S. Customs and Border Protection, 90 K Street NE, 10th Floor, Washington, DC 20229-1177. Grounds: ACE Protest Module is a searchable automated system; producing an extract is a search, not record creation (§552(a)(3)(C)–(D)).
   - **OGIS mediation** (free, non-binding): Office of Government Information Services, NARA — available any time after response.
   - Litigation in federal district court is the last resort (unlikely to be worth it; note but don't plan for it).
7. **Log it:** record tracking number, submission date, and deadlines in the project tracker; save the acknowledgment PDF to `docs/foia/`.

## (c) Five content/product uses for the data

1. **"State of the Customs Protest" annual report** — flagship SEO/PR asset: first-ever public grant/denial rates by issue category. Trade press (JOC, American Shipper, Sandler Travis / ST&R newsletter ecosystem) will cite it; every citation is a backlink and broker-credibility signal.
2. **Grant-rate priors in the product** — show "protests on classification issues were granted ~X% of the time in FY2024" next to each TariffCheck finding, turning a drafted remedy package into an expected-value number the broker can show their client.
3. **Broker sales collateral** — one-pager for outreach: "CBP decided N protests last year; the median took M days; here's how a pre-verified package moves you to the fast side of that distribution." Concrete numbers beat generic pitch decks in broker conversations.
4. **AFR strategy guidance** — if AFR vs standard-review outcome rates differ materially, publish a decision guide ("when to request Further Review") and encode it in the drafting flow as a recommendation, which is a defensible, data-backed feature no rival has.
5. **Benchmark API / calculator** — a free "protest odds" widget (issue category + port + amount in → historical disposition stats out) as a lead magnet on tariffcheck-zeta.vercel.app; gate the detailed breakdowns behind the Broker Audit tier.
