## 1. Target profile

**Who:** independent US customs brokerage firms, ~2–50 people, NOT owned by a forwarder-competitor (excludes Flexport, Expeditors, Kuehne+Nagel, DHL, DSV, C.H. Robinson subsidiaries, and anything recently acquired by them). Decision-maker: owner / president / principal LCB. Bonus fit: firms on **NetCHB** or **CargoWise** (we have an integration story for both).

**Why they buy:** Flexport-style digital forwarders now give importers free "audit your broker" tooling. An independent broker's moat is trust + expertise; TariffCheck lets them run the audit *first*, under their own name.

### LinkedIn search recipes (exact strings)

1. People: `"Licensed Customs Broker" AND ("owner" OR "president" OR "principal")` — Location: United States; Industry: Transportation, Logistics, Supply Chain and Storage; check company size 2–50 per profile.
2. People: `"LCB" AND "customs brokerage" AND ("founder" OR "co-founder" OR "managing partner")`
3. Company: `"customs brokerage"` — Company size filters "1-10" + "11-50"; run per metro: LA, NYC, Houston, Laredo, Miami.
4. Company: `"customs house broker" OR "customhouse broker"` — size 1-10 + 11-50; the older phrasing self-selects legacy family firms.
5. People (software angle): keywords `"NetCHB" OR "CargoWise"` + `"customs"`, Title: broker OR compliance — their employers are the integration-ready list.
6. No-Sales-Navigator fallback (Google): `site:linkedin.com/company "customs brokerage" "2-10 employees"` (and `"11-50 employees"`).

## 4. Objection handling (one page)

**"My clients already trust me — I don't need this."**
Trust is exactly what's at stake. Flexport-style tools put a free second opinion in your client's inbox whether you like it or not. When their tool flags an entry, the conversation starts with you on defense. Run the same scan first and the conversation starts with you handing them recovered duties. The audit is happening either way; the only question is whose letterhead is on it.

**"Liability — what if your AI is wrong and I file it?"**
Fair, and it's why we built it the way we did. Every finding is deterministically verified — checked against the current HTSUS, CROSS rulings, and CBP regulations, not just model output — and we publish our false-positive rate. Nothing goes to CBP without your review: you get a drafted package, your licensed broker signs or spikes it. We never file, so the professional judgment — and the client relationship — stays yours. That's a design choice, not a limitation.

**"I don't need another SaaS subscription."**
Neither do we — that's why the pilot costs nothing for 90 days on your real portfolio. If the scan finds nothing worth filing, walk away, no invoice. If it does, $795/month flat with no success fee means one modest recovered PSC typically covers the year. Compare that to the per-recovery cuts the audit-firm alternatives take.

**"We already do this manually when a client asks."**
And you're good at it — on the entries somebody thought to question. The scan covers the whole book: every entry, every HTS line, Chapter 99 layering, exclusions, valuation, origin — including the refunds nobody asked about because nobody saw them. Manual review finds what you look for; a full-portfolio pass finds what you didn't know to look for. Your team still makes every call; they just start from a drafted package instead of a blank Form 19.
