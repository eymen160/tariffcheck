## 1. Market context (changed materially since the July 12 audit)

- **SCOTUS struck down IEEPA tariffs Feb 20, 2026** (6–3, *Learning Resources v. Trump*); ~$166B refundable across ~53M entries / ~330K importers. CBP's **CAPE** portal: Phase 1 Apr 20, Phase 2 Jun 29, Phase 3 (finally-liquidated, CIT filers only) due **late July 2026**. ~$104B authorized, ~$71B paid by Jun 29. Government appealed the CIT refund order in June — refund mechanics could still shift.
- **CAPE commoditizes IEEPA refunds only.** Misclassification, missed FTA/USMCA, 301 errors, valuation — our core wedge — still require §1514 protests / PSCs. CAPE trained 330K importers to expect refunds; the durable market is non-IEEPA recovery.
- **Section 122 10% global tariff expires by statute July 24, 2026 (this week)** — a content/news moment.
- **Section 301: 178 exclusions expire Nov 10, 2026** — a hard Q4 2026 audit event.
- **De minimis suspended for all non-postal modes Jun 24, 2026; statutorily abolished Jul 1, 2027** (OBBBA, court-proof). ~1.3B packages/yr now need entries → broker volume crush (Amari/Alchemize feed on this).
- **USMCA joint review Jul 1, 2026: US declined to extend** → annual reviews until 2036. FTA-claim substantiation (our 1520(d) routing) gets *more* valuable.
- **PSC NCAP test modified** (FR 2026-13574), effective Aug 5, 2026.
- Thomson Reuters 2026 Global Trade Report: only **7%** of orgs use software specifically for tariff-change management; 72% call volatility their top challenge.

## 4. Feature-gap matrix — top 10 (severity × prevalence; repo-verified Jul 21)

Note: two July-12 audit findings are **stale** — Section 301 is now line-level (10,391 subheadings, `section301.json`) and liquidation-status intake shipped in v3.1.

1. **Persistence/DB** (Neon Postgres path known to team) — prerequisite for #2–#7; every competitor with a login has it. Effort M. **Fix first.**
2. **ACE ES-003 ingestion as a real flow** — Caspian/Gaia/Flexport/Pax all audit *filed entries*; invoice-only auditing misses the 7501-vs-truth delta where refunds live. Our batch schema already accepts every ES-003 field; the gap is file ingestion (S–M), not the data model. Verdict: **critical**.
3. **7501 PDF parsing** — Reform does exactly this; brokers hold 7501s, not CSVs; we already have a PDF pipeline. **Highest leverage-per-effort (S–M).**
4. **Continuous monitoring / tariff-change alerts** — 6+ competitors claim it; **our pricing page sells it but it isn't built**. Without it $349/mo is a project fee, not SaaS. M–L (needs DB + cron).
5. **Multi-client broker workspace / seats** — the $1,750 tier promises multi-client catalogs that can't exist statelessly. M–L (after DB).
6. **Batch >100 lines** — broker "90-day scan" is thousands of lines; serverless timeout is the real cap. S–M.
7. **White-label drafts** — sold on pricing page, not built; cheap (S–M); brokers resell at $500–2,000/filing.
8. **SOC 2 (+ SSO, audit trail)** — Caspian is Type II certified. On a timer: start Type I when first broker pilot converts.
9. **User-facing CROSS citations** — Quickcode owns this; we sell it in Pro and have the 500-ruling corpus internally. M.
10. **PSC as ABI-transmittable file** (vs our letter draft) — Reform hands brokers a transmission-ready file. M.

**Integrity flag:** three pricing-page features are sold-but-not-built (alerts, CROSS citations, white-label) — a real risk for a brand built on the honesty ledger. Fix or relabel "coming soon."

**Zero-effort prod fixes still open:** set `LEADS_WEBHOOK_URL` (leads currently die in logs) and `ALLOWED_ORIGINS` (CORS `*` fallback).

**Strategic choices to KEEP (not gaps):** non-filer posture (channel safety), no drawback (refer out), US-only, published pricing + published FP methodology, free HTS lookup, no ABI *filing* (ingestion is the part to build).

## 5. Pricing & GTM findings

Verified anchors: Quickcode broker tier **$749/mo unlimited seats** (the number every small brokerage will quote back at us); Gaia $99–$1,399/mo tiers; Caspian protests **2% of claim / $100K cap**; KlearNow **$100/entry**; Pax 1–30% declining contingency; drawback industry 10–25%; IEEPA legal contingency 15–25% (publicly criticized); broker protest services $200–400/hr or $500–2,000/filing; CargoWise now **$9.95/customs entry, unlimited users**; AmLaw partners ~$1,000/hr.

Recommendations (each anchored to a datapoint):

1. **Add a broker entry tier at ~$749–$899/mo** (capped white-label drafts or 3 seats); keep $1,750 as "Broker Unlimited." Quickcode's $749 anchor otherwise cedes the small-brokerage segment. $1M plan gets easier as 40 firms mixing $9K + $22K.
2. **Never per-seat for brokers — price per entry-block / per white-label draft.** The market's mental model is per-transaction ($9.95 CargoWise). A "$69/draft" overage reads against the $500–2,000 brokers bill per filing — a 10x visible margin, our best sales slide.
3. **Reprice/reframe the success fee.** "10% flat" was defensible vs 15–25% legal contingency, but **Caspian's published 2%/claim ($100K cap) resets the protest-specific benchmark**. Options: match with a capped low-% on protest recoveries, or lean fully on SaaS + waived fee when the broker files (our channel story anyway). Do not lead with 10% against Caspian in importer deals.
4. **Keep free lookup generous; gate the protest draft, not the audit.** Flexport gives a full audit away free — the monetizable artifact is the verified §1514 package, which nobody gives away.
5. **Ship a CAPE flat-fee SKU now** (e.g., $149 CAPE-readiness audit per claim batch) — brokers charge $500–2,000/declaration, ~$100B still unpaid, Phase 3 opens within days. Time-boxed.
6. **Channel: copy the Quickcode×Magaya embed play** on the platforms Quickcode hasn't locked — NetCHB (public WSDL, 700+ brokers, SMB-heavy) and CargoWise marketplace; apply to Magaya partner program; NCBFAA 2027 exhibitor cycle (Amari already exhibits).

## 6. Whitespace — top 5 by leverage

1. **Published accuracy + ATLAS score (effort LOW, ~1 week).** Open benchmark ATLAS (arXiv 2509.18400): 18,731 CROSS rulings; best fine-tuned model **40% @ 10-digit**. Competitors publish only marketing numbers (Gaia "92%" / n=15 CBLE stunt; Quickcode "nearly 100%", no methodology; Caspian/Amari/Pax/Trava/Reform: nothing). Claimable category: *first commercial product with published accuracy on public ground truth, an FP rate, and a deterministic no-hallucination guarantee.* They can't follow without exposing their own claims.
2. **HS 2028 readiness tool (effort LOW-MED, ~18-month head start).** WCO correlation tables published Apr 2026; 428 new subheadings, 172 deleted, ~8% amended, effective Jan 1, 2028. Zero product announcements from any AI-native competitor. Free "will your codes survive 2028" checker + per-code programmatic SEO pages; USITC's final HTS 2028 won't land until late 2027.
3. **IEEPA/CAPE long-tail self-serve (effort MED, time-boxed).** Micro-importers aren't filing (don't know they're eligible); CPAs only serve big clients. "Does CBP owe you money" estimator → CAPE-ready verified package → converts to recurring audit.
4. **Protest-outcome data loop + FOIA (effort LOW to start).** No public CBP protest statistics exist; ACE Protest Module returns granted/denied to filers; nobody does outcome-loop learning. File FOIA for FY22–26 protest dispositions by issue code this month → data moat + killer content ("X% of classification protests are granted").
5. **Broker white-label via NetCHB/Magaya partner programs (effort MED).** No classification-audit tool is embedded in any broker-software marketplace today; our non-filer posture is the structural admission ticket rivals lack. Secondary: white-label duty-audit reports for small CPA firms (Baker Tilly/Aprio only serve the top end, manually).
